about indra gandhi

Indira Gandhi (1917–1984) was the first woman Prime Minister of India and one of the country’s most influential leaders. She was the daughter of Jawaharlal Nehru and became Prime Minister in 1966. Indira Gandhi is remembered for her bold decisions, including the Green Revolution that made India self-sufficient in food grains and the 1971 Bangladesh Liberation War. She was known for her strong leadership style and centralised power. She served as Prime Minister for more than 15 years in total until her assassination in 1984.

FAMILY

Indira Gandhi belonged to the famous Nehru family of India. She was the only daughter of Jawaharlal Nehru, the first Prime Minister of India. She married Feroze Gandhi and had two sons, Rajiv Gandhi and Sanjay Gandhi. Rajiv Gandhi later became Prime Minister, and today his children Rahul Gandhi and Priyanka Gandhi Vadra are active in Indian politics.
